The engine · plain steps
1
Find the baseline
The lowest sustained load between 02:00–05:00 is the always-on floor: servers, security, fridges, standby. Everything above it is discretionary.
2
Read the calendar, not a sensor
Occupancy comes from the building's known schedule — operating hours, weekends, holidays. No occupancy hardware needed.
3
Attribute the remainder
Load above baseline splits by time-of-day physics: the cooling curve drives HVAC, dark-hour excess flags lighting, the rest scales with occupancy as office load.
4
Flag what shouldn't be there
Any HVAC or lighting estimated while the calendar says "empty" is waste — costed instantly at the live TNB tariff.
The engine · the real formula
inputs: total_kw, timestamp, schedule baseline = min(total_kw between 02:00–05:00) remainder = total_kw − baseline occ = schedule(hour, weekday) if occupied: lighting = remainder × 0.18 hvac = remainder × 0.55 else (empty by calendar): lighting = remainder × 0.80 ← waste hvac = remainder × 0.70 ← waste office = remainder − hvac − lighting waste_rm = (hvac + lighting while empty) × tariff
